VII. IELTS Syllabus & Official Information

About IELTS

The International English Language Testing System (IELTS) is a globally recognized English proficiency test, required for study, work, and migration. There are two main types: Academic (for higher education/professional registration) and General Training (for work/migration). The test is jointly owned by the British Council, IDP IELTS, and Cambridge University Press & Assessment.

  • Total Test Time: 2 hours 45 minutes
  • Sections: Listening, Reading, Writing, Speaking
  • Test Modes: Paper-based or Computer-based
  • Scoring: Band scale 0–9 (no pass/fail)

IELTS Syllabus (2025)

  • Listening: 30 minutes, 40 questions, 4 audio recordings (conversations, monologues, academic discussions, lectures). Accents: British, Australian, Canadian, American.
    Tips: Use preview time, focus on keywords, answers in capitals.
  • Reading: 60 minutes, 40 questions, 3 long passages (Academic: journals, books, newspapers; General: real-life texts).
    Question types: MCQ, True/False/Not Given, Matching, Sentence Completion, etc.
    Tips: Skim for main ideas, scan for details, manage time.
  • Writing: 60 minutes, 2 tasks.
    • Academic: Task 1 – Describe visual data (chart, graph, table); Task 2 – Formal essay (argument, opinion, problem-solving).
    • General Training: Task 1 – Write a letter; Task 2 – Essay (similar to Academic).
    Tips: Allocate 20 min for Task 1, 40 min for Task 2, stick to word count, write a clear conclusion.
  • Speaking: 11–14 minutes, face-to-face or virtual interview.
    • Part 1: Introduction & basic questions (4–5 min)
    • Part 2: Cue card topic (1 min prep, 2 min talk)
    • Part 3: Follow-up discussion (5–6 min)
    Tips: Be fluent, don't memorize, focus on clarity, ask for clarification if needed.

Key Facts & Preparation

  • Listening and Speaking sections are the same for both Academic and General Training; Reading and Writing differ.
  • No limit to the number of attempts.
  • Most universities/immigration require a band score between 6.0 and 7.5.
  • Preparation: Use official IELTS materials, mock tests, and practice regularly.
  • For more details and sample questions, visit the official IELTS website.

IX. IELTS Destinations & Global Opportunities

Canada

IELTS is accepted for study, work, and migration. Required for university admission and Express Entry immigration.

USA

Over 3,400 institutions accept IELTS for study, including Ivy League universities.

United Kingdom

Required for UK Visas & Immigration (UKVI), and accepted by all UK universities and colleges.

Australia

Accepted for study, work, and migration. Required for skilled migration and university admission.

New Zealand

Accepted for university admission, skilled migration, and work visas.

IELTS Key Facts for Students

  • IELTS is accepted in over 140 countries and by 11,000+ institutions worldwide.
  • It is the preferred test for migration to Australia, Canada, New Zealand, and the UK.
  • IELTS Academic is for higher education and professional registration; General Training is for work and migration.
  • IELTS scores are valid for 2 years.
  • Test available up to 4 times a month in 1,600+ locations globally.
  • Computer-delivered and paper-based options available.
  • For more, visit the official IELTS website.
